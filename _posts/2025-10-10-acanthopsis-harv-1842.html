Conclusion

Acanthopsis Harv. (1842), commonly known as Chinese Abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to southeastern China found at low elevations in the lower alpine zone up to 6000'. This shrub belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is named after Dr. Clark Abel, an 18th-century naturalist. The genus is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom from early summer to fall, lasting an extended period. The shrub prefers moist, acidic, well-drained soils in full sun to part shade. It is relatively easy to grow, drought tolerant once established, and deer and rabbit tolerant. The arching branches can reach 5-8 feet tall and 3-5 feet wide, making it suitable for use as a specimen plant or in borders, foundations, or as a pollinator garden. Propagation is possible through softwood or semi-hardwood cuttings. While it was previously used in commercial cultivars, it is now appreciated for its natural beauty and nectar-producing flowers that attract butterflies.
