Care & Cultivation

Afzelia care and cultivation guide depends on specific species, but here's general guidance based on information about the genus:


Site Selection: Plant Afzelia in well-draining, acidic to neutral soil. Native species thrive in fertile loam but tolerate a range of soil types as long as drainage is good. Avoid areas prone to waterlogging which can cause root rot.
Soil: While not strictly *required*, well-draining soil is ideal. The natural habitat includes riverbanks and rocky slopes, suggesting a preference for soils with some organic matter and drainage. Avoid heavy clay soils.
Planting: Dig a hole twice the width and depth of the root ball. Gently remove the plant from its container, loosen any tangled roots, and refill the hole with the prepared soil, packing lightly to secure the root ball. Water thoroughly after planting.
Watering: Young plants need consistent moisture, especially during establishment. Once mature, they are relatively drought-tolerant. Water deeply but infrequently, ensuring the soil around the root zone stays consistently moist but not waterlogged. In hot, dry climates, supplemental watering may be necessary.
Light: Full sun to partial shade is generally preferred. Full sun promotes good growth and flowering. Shade can reduce leaf scorch but may decrease flower production.
Fertilizing: Afzelia benefits from regular fertilization, especially during the growing season (spring and summer). Use a balanced, slow-release fertilizer applied according to package instructions. Avoid over-fertilizing, which can cause foliage burn. A liquid fertilizer applied weekly during active growth can also be used.
Propagation: The most common method is through root cuttings. Take cuttings in early spring or late summer when growth is active.
